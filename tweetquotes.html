#Recipe: Tweet Quotes

Give your readers timely, relevant quotes to share on Twitter.

Tweet quotes enable your readers to share content on your behalf, increasing your distribution on Twitter. And by adding Twitter Cards you site, you offer a more compelling experience to Twitter users, as well as gain insight to help optimize your conversion and traffic.

This recipe can be built into your website or CMS system in under 10 minutes. It uses and free fully supported Twitter tools, including:

  • Web Intents
  • Twitter Cards

Step 1: Learn about Web Intents  

Step 2: Learn about Web Intents

A Web Intent helps readers share your message on Twitter, without ever leaving your site.

A Web Intent is a parameterized URL that, when clicked, pre-populates your message in a Tweet. The most popular parameters are:

  • text. The text/summary quote you want your readers to share.
  • url. The URL of your content on your site.
  • via. The Twitter handle associated with your site.
  • related. A comma separated list of Twitter handles related to your site.

Below is a sample Web Intent URL:

Copy 
https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?text=This%is%20a%20tweet%20quote&url=http%3A%2F%2Fdeveloper.twitter.com&via=twitterdev

If you want additional customization options or power tips, read more on Twitter Web Intents.

Step 3: Compose a Twitter Card

Twitter Cards bring rich images and summaries to a bigger audience.

When your readers Tweet links to your articles, their Tweets will also have the image and summary you specify.

Add the below HTML to the HEAD section of your website:

Copy 
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image" />
<meta name="twitter:site" content="@flickr" />
<meta name="twitter:title" content="publ(ic) mark(et)" />
<meta name="twitter:description" content="Pike Place Market  Seattle, WA USA" />
<meta name="twitter:image" content="http://farm4.staticflickr.com/3361/3433069736_7193f22ab4_z.jpg" />
<meta name="twitter:url" content="https://www.flickr.com/photos/secret_canadian/3433069736//" />

For advanced options, read the Twitter Card Documentation.
