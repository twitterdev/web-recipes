#Recipe: Hashtag Campaign

Engage your users and followers with a hashtag campaign on your site.

A hashtag campaign can be used for:

  • Raising awareness about a cause or event
  • Sourcing photos or engagement from your audience
  • Hosting a Twitter-based Q&A session
  • Running contests or promotions

This recipe can easily be built into your website or CMS system. It uses and free fully supported Twitter tools, including:

  • Collections
  • Tweet Buttons
  • Twitter Cards
  • TweetDeck

The code and the how-to for you to get started (including this recipe) is all below.

Walkthrough: Overview

This section walks through the steps of creating a hashtag campaign in under 10 minutes.

The steps are:

  1. Choose a hashtag
  2. Add a Tweet button to your website
  3. Create a Collection
  4. Add the Collection to your website
  5. Curate the Collection

Through this walkthrough, you'll also learn a bit about TweetDeck, a free power tool provided by Twitter to help you track, organize and manage Tweets.

Step 1: Choose a Hashtag  

Step 1: Choose a Hashtag

The hashtag is the cornerstone of an effective campaign.

Pick the right one to represent your campaign and connect with your audience. And keep it simple with the following:

  • Is memorable. So your customers can easily recall them.
  • Is short. So the hashtag can be easily typed on a mobile keypad. Alphabetic is best.
  • Is not already in use. To see if another brand is using the same hashtag, search for it on Twitter.

Some great hashtags include #AskObama, #HeForShe and #DoveBeautifulAge. If you need more help, read this great post on our Twitter blog on "How to Choose a Hashtag".

Step 2: Add a Tweet button to your website

Engage your audience directly from your website with a Tweet button.

The easiest way to create a hashtag button is by using our Button Builder. Visit the publish.twitter.com site and click on the "Twitter Buttons" option to select a Hashtag button. Be sure to remember the following:

  • Add your hashtag to drive the campaign.
  • Under the Customization options, include a URL, so people click through to your website.
  • Under the Customization options, specify your @handle via the "Recommend" option to drive followers.
Upon completing the builder, you should have HTML code similar to the following to add to your website source.

<a href="https://twitter.com/intent/tweet?button_hashtag=Camping" class="twitter-hashtag-button" data-size="large" data-related="dickc,twitter" data-via="camping"  data-url="http://bit.ly/SOMETHING">Tweet #Camping</a>

Step 3: Create a Collection

Collections let you curate and display the best Tweets from your audience.

The easiest way to create a Collection is via TweetDeck. Follow the below steps:
  1. Login via TweetDeck
  2. Click on "Add Column" in the left hand sidebar and choose "Collections"
  3. Click on the "Create Collection" button. Your new collection will be in the rightmost column
  4. Specify a name and description as desired.
Once your Collection has been created, you can add it to your website in the next step.

Step 4: Add a Collection to your website

A Collection Widget displays curated Tweets on your website.

Follow the below steps to generate code for your website:

  1. Open the Twitter Widget builder
  2. Paste the URL to your Collection
  3. Specify additional Customization options, as desired.
  4. The page should now show HTML, similar to the one below. Add this HTML to your website source.

Step 5: Curate the Collection

Use TweetDeck to pick the best Tweets to display on your website.

Now that your campaign is configured on your website, use TweetDeck to add Tweets into your Collection. Follow the below best practices to discover and display Tweets effectively:

  1. Search for Tweets in TweetDeck by hashtag.
  2. If you find a Tweet you like, click on "..." and then "Add to collection" to add it to your Collection.
  3. Include a mix of Tweets with and without images for variety.
Once you've added a couple Tweets, refresh your campaign website to see those Tweets appearing.
